## Maricopa Lions Club — Information Sheet

The **Maricopa Lions Club** is a 501(c)(3) charitable nonprofit organization.

The Maricopa Lions Club is part of **Lions Clubs International**, the world’s largest service club organization. Founded in 1917, Lions have served communities worldwide through humanitarian service and a shared commitment to making a difference. Today, more than 1.4 million members serve in over 49,000 clubs across 200+ countries and geographic areas. Lions are recognized globally for their dedication to serving people who are blind or visually impaired.

**Website:**  
https://www.maricopalions.org

---

## Club History & Purpose

The Maricopa Lions Club was formed in **March 2015** to serve individuals who are blind, deaf, visually impaired, hearing impaired, and other community groups. The club is built on fellowship and service, with a focus on meeting the needs of the local community.

## Organizational Naming & Usage

### Lions Clubs International

- **First usage:** *Lions Clubs International*  
- **Second usage:** *Our organization*  

**Do not use** “LCI,” “Lions,” or “Lions Clubs” as a replacement for *Lions Clubs International*.

---

### Lions Clubs International Foundation

- **First usage:** *Lions Clubs International Foundation (LCIF)*  
- **Second usage:** *LCIF*  
- **Third usage:** *Our global foundation*, *Your foundation*

---

### Multi-Entity Rule

When more than one Lions entity appears in the same piece, **do not use** “our organization.”  
Use the **full formal name** instead to avoid ambiguity.

---

## Voice

When creating marketing materials, the voice should reflect the **Maricopa Lions Club**, while remaining consistent with **Lions Clubs International brand standards**.

This means:
- Short and to the point  
- Active voice over passive voice  
- Third person by default  
- First or second person may be used when appropriate  

---

## Tone

Tone varies by context, but always reflects professionalism and care.

**Primary tone qualities**
- Trustworthy  
- Empathetic  
- Informative  

When speaking about the organization and its members, a more informal tone is acceptable.  
When telling service stories or writing about the people we serve, language must be thoughtful and respectful.

**LCIF-specific tone guidance**  
LCIF communications should always maintain an appropriate level of seriousness to reflect:
- The gravity of the needs being addressed  
- The dignity of the people we serve  
- Sincere appreciation when requesting contributions  

---

## Writing Style

### Headlines and Subheadlines
- Headlines are high-level, emotional, and engaging  
- Subheadlines clarify or expand on the headline  
- Use sentence case  
- Include end punctuation when the line is a complete sentence  

### Body Copy
- Strong language delivers key messages and invites readers in  
- Vary sentence length to create rhythm and flow  
- Follow AP style as a baseline  
- Rules may be intentionally broken for effect (e.g., fragments, starting sentences with conjunctions)

### Calls to Action
- Strong, clear, and inviting  
- Visually emphasized so they are easy to find  
- Clearly communicate value and benefit  

---

### Accuracy and Claims

Marketing language may emphasize impact and community benefit, but:
- Do not invent statistics, outcomes, or claims
- Do not imply endorsements or partnerships beyond those listed
- All factual statements should be grounded in the Information Sheet or verified sources
